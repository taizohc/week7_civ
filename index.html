Sala de Operaciones — Santiago de Chile

Under President Salvador Allende, Chile built the world's first attempt at a real-time networked national economy — not a free market, not Soviet central planning, but a third way.

British cyberneticist Stafford Beer designed a system linking over 500 state factories by telex. Each day, production data flowed into Santiago. Managers could see anomalies and intervene — fast enough to matter.

The operations room seated seven people in swivelling fibreglass chairs, surrounded by screens. No keyboards. No paper. Just real-time data and decisions. It was destroyed in the Pinochet coup, September 1973.
